The Mission

To craft stoneware that anchors the daily ritual of eating.

We reject the fragility of fine china and the soullessness of factory ceramics. Our mission is to make plates, bowls, and mugs that are meant to be used, stacked, scraped, and washed—durable companions designed for real, messy, beautiful life.

02

The Vision

A dining table entirely free of mass production.

We envision a return to intentional consumption, where homes and restaurants serve meals on vessels that hold the thumbprints of their makers. We want to prove that small-batch, human-scale craftsmanship can still thrive in a world obsessed with speed.

How we got here

Nine years,
told in five firings.

2017

One second-hand wheel

Meera Nair quits a design job, buys a wobbling Shimpo wheel off a retiring potter, and starts throwing in her parents' garage. First sale: six uneven chai cups to a neighbour.

2019

Our own kiln, finally

After two years of renting kiln space across town, we install our own 60-cubic-foot gas kiln. Firing No. 001 is logged in a notebook we still keep on the shelf.

2021

Pottery Lane

We move into the Indiranagar studio — three wheels, a glaze corner, and a wall of drying shelves. Two makers become four, and Saturday workshops begin.

2024

Restaurants come knocking

Terra Kitchen commissions a 240-piece tasting-menu line. Word travels through chef circles; today 28 restaurants across India plate on KlinCraft stoneware.

Today

Batch No. 015 is in the kiln

Five makers, one kiln, 52 firings a year — and still small enough that every piece passes through human hands at least nine times before it reaches your table.

A factory can make a thousand identical plates before lunch. It took me a year to make six that I liked. I've stopped seeing that as a disadvantage.

Meera Nair Founder & lead thrower

Our values, stated backwards

Things we
refuse to do.

Most studios list what they believe in. We find it more honest to list what we've turned down — because every "no" below has cost us money at some point, and we'd say it again.

No slip-cast moulds

Moulds would triple our output overnight. They'd also make every piece a copy. Each form is thrown individually on the wheel — it's the whole point.

No mystery glazes

Every glaze is mixed in-house from raw minerals and tested for food safety. We publish the full material list for each — ask and we'll send it.

No landfill clay

Trimmings go back to the wedging table; cracked greenware is reclaimed; glaze-fired seconds are sold at the studio, never binned.

No order we can't fire ourselves

We've declined orders that would force us to outsource production. If it doesn't pass through this kiln, it doesn't carry the KlinCraft stamp.
