From mud to mealtime

Five stages. Eleven days.
Zero shortcuts.

Day 1 · Wedge

Preparing clay

Local stoneware clay is wedged by hand to push out air pockets — the silent killers of kiln firings.

Day 1–2 · Throw

On the wheel

Each form is thrown individually. Weight, rim, and curve are checked against the batch master.

Day 3–4 · Trim

Trim & stamp

Leather-hard pieces are trimmed, footed, and stamped with the KlinCraft seal and batch number.

Day 6–7 · Glaze

Dipped by hand

After a bisque firing, every piece is hand-dipped in our small-batch, food-safe glazes and plates.

Day 9–11 · Fire

1280°C, 14 hours

The glaze firing vitrifies the clay — chip-resistant, watertight, and ready for decades of dinners and sets.

The Glaze Library

Six glazes, mixed
in our own buckets.

We blend every glaze in-house from raw minerals — no commercial pre-mixes. Tap a swatch to see it on the plate. Each one reacts differently to the kiln, so your piece will carry its own pattern of pools and breaks.

The Studio

A three-wheel workshop
in Bengaluru.

KlinCraft started in 2017 with one second-hand wheel and a borrowed kiln. Today we're a team of five makers producing dinnerware for homes and restaurants across India — still in batches small enough that every piece passes through human hands at least nine times.

Seconds and kiln-kissed pieces are sold at the studio every first Saturday, and the off-cuts of clay go straight back into the wedging table. Almost nothing leaves this room as waste.
